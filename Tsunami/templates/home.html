Understanding Tsunamis

Tsunamis are large ocean waves caused by underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides. They can travel across entire ocean basins and can cause widespread devastation when they reach land.

Historical Tsunamis

Throughout history, significant tsunamis have impacted coastal communities. Notable events include the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami and the 2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami in Japan, which resulted in thousands of casualties and massive destruction.

Preparation and Safety

Being prepared for a tsunami is crucial for coastal residents. Awareness of evacuation routes, having emergency kits ready, and staying informed through local alerts can save lives during such natural disasters.
