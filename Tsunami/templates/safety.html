Tsunami Safety Information

Tsunamis are powerful natural disasters that can cause devastating impacts on coastal communities. It is crucial to be informed and prepared to ensure your safety and the safety of others. Here are some essential safety tips and guidelines to follow before, during, and after a tsunami.

Before a Tsunami

  • Know your risk. If you live in a coastal area, familiarize yourself with local tsunami evacuation routes.
  • Prepare an emergency kit that includes essentials such as food, water, first aid supplies, and medications.
  • Develop a family emergency plan, including a designated meeting place and communication strategies.

During a Tsunami

  • If you are in a tsunami evacuation zone, move to higher ground immediately.
  • Stay away from the coast. Do not go to the beach to watch the tsunami.
  • Follow evacuation orders issued by local authorities.

After a Tsunami

  • Listen to emergency information and alerts from local authorities.
  • Stay away from damaged areas and do not return until it is safe to do so.
  • Report any injuries or emergencies to local emergency services.

Additional Resources

For more detailed information about tsunami preparedness, visit Ready.gov.
