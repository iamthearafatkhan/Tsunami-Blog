CNN — It begins with a roaring sound not dissimilar from a freight train or a Boeing 747 in takeoff. Then, a wall of water, with an unimaginable force that can flatten an entire building in seconds. If you survive the initial blow, you now face an unrelenting pounding of wave after wave, and like a pinball machine, you are being battered by debris, uprooted trees and cars that share the same violent trajectory as you.

History of Tsunamis

Notable Tsunamis

  • 2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami

    Year: 2004

    Date: December 26, 2004

    Location: Indian Ocean, affecting countries like Indonesia, Sri Lanka, India, and Thailand.

    Destruction: Over 230,000 people were killed, and millions were displaced. Coastal towns were completely destroyed, with waves reaching heights of up to 30 meters (100 feet).

  • 2011 Tōhoku Tsunami

    Year: 2011

    Date: March 11, 2011

    Location: Off the coast of Japan, particularly affecting the Tōhoku region.

    Destruction: The tsunami caused widespread devastation, resulting in approximately 18,500 deaths, damage to infrastructure, and triggering the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster.

  • 1960 Valdivia Tsunami

    Year: 1960

    Date: May 22, 1960

    Location: Chile, but it also affected Hawaii, Japan, and the Philippines.

    Destruction: Triggered by the most powerful earthquake ever recorded, the tsunami resulted in over 5,700 deaths and extensive damage along the coast of Chile.

  • 1946 Aleutian Islands Tsunami

    Year: 1946

    Date: April 1, 1946

    Location: Aleutian Islands, Alaska, and affecting Hawaii.

    Destruction: This tsunami killed 165 people in Hawaii and caused significant damage to coastal communities.

  • 1883 Krakatoa Tsunami

    Year: 1883

    Date: August 27, 1883

    Location: Sunda Strait between Java and Sumatra, Indonesia.

    Destruction: The eruption of Krakatoa caused tsunamis that destroyed over 165 coastal villages, resulting in more than 36,000 deaths.

  • 2009 Samoa Tsunami

    Year: 2009

    Date: September 29, 2009

    Location: Samoa and American Samoa.

    Destruction: The tsunami caused significant damage, resulting in 200 deaths and devastating coastal areas.

  • 2018 Sunda Strait Tsunami

    Year: 2018

    Date: December 22, 2018

    Location: Indonesia, particularly the islands of Java and Sumatra.

    Destruction: Triggered by the eruption of Anak Krakatau, the tsunami killed over 400 people and caused extensive damage to coastal towns.

2024 Tsunami Events

  • 2024 Tsunami in the Pacific Ocean

    Year: 2024

    Date: March 15, 2024

    Location: Near the Tonga Islands in the South Pacific Ocean.

    Destruction: A 7.8 magnitude earthquake triggered a tsunami that affected several Pacific islands. While there were no immediate reports of widespread damage, the potential for significant disruption exists due to the geographical layout of the region.

  • 2024 Tsunami in the Caribbean

    Year: 2024

    Date: August 22, 2024

    Location: Off the coast of Puerto Rico.

    Destruction: Triggered by a powerful undersea earthquake, the tsunami caused flooding in coastal areas, resulting in damage to infrastructure and evacuation of residents from low-lying regions.
